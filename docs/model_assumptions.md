# Model Assumptions

## Core Assumptions

1. Media effects are non-negative unless there is strong evidence otherwise.
2. Media effects persist beyond the exposure week through adstock.
3. Marginal returns decline as spend increases.
4. Non-media demand drivers are material in healthcare and must be controlled.
5. Short observational histories can produce unstable estimates without priors or calibration.

## Channel-Specific Assumptions

| Channel | Expected Lag | Saturation Risk | Prior Logic |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Google Search | Short | Medium-high | Demand capture and high intent |
| Meta | Medium | Medium | Prospecting plus retargeting |
| YouTube | Long | Medium | Awareness and delayed response |
| Display | Medium | High | Support channel with modest direct lift |

## Healthcare Assumptions

Healthcare lead generation differs from retail or SaaS:
- conversion quality matters more than raw volume;
- appointment capacity can constrain observed response;
- privacy and compliance constraints may limit user-level attribution;
- call tracking can break or shift measurement quality;
- seasonality can be clinically and commercially meaningful.

## Interpretation Boundaries

The model estimates plausible media contribution under assumptions. It does not prove causal impact by itself. Experimental calibration improves credibility.
